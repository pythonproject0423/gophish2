Phishing Attack Prevention Campaign

Phishing attacks are an ongoing thing and the companies who protect themselves against phishing attacks keep them at bay. Since, small businesses do not take the appropriate steps to protect against phishing and they do not respond like they should when cyber attacks happen. They lose completely in terms of finance and reputation and they do at times end up under legal issues. But why?

We see that business owners understand the risk and majority percentage responded that they’re concerned about cyber attacks. However, half of businesses owners mention finances as the reason for not taking a proactive stance against cyber attacks. They miss one key fact: Human error, not infrastructure, causes these vulnerabilities.

Businesses miss addressing the human element to protect against phishing, and invest in cyber insurance, installation of IDS intrusion detection, hire cybersecurity experts, but without addressing the human factor, their efforts fall short.

This is specially true when it comes to phishing scams, a type of social engineering attack that continues to grow.

Common Phishing Scams

Email phishing

Email attacks can happen in several forms. Some attacks are disguised in malware as email attachments while others direct users to fraudulent websites. But the worst ones are phishing for financial information through legitimate-looking offers or notices.

Search engine phishing

In this kind of attacks, scammers rely on users’ trust of search engine results to create fraudulent websites

Malvertising

In case of or malicious advertising, a scammer uses malicious code into online ads which are click away from the users expose device to Malware

Vishing

In this method VoIP is used to spoof the caller ID of legitimate institutions. In a vishing attack, attackers call pretending to be someone else to gain access to sensitive information from the target.

Prevent Phishing Attacks

You can not stop the phishing attack, but can avoid to be a victim through following to preventive measures.

  1. Employee Training
  2. Security Policies

It only takes one mistake by an employee to cost a business financial loss or reputation damage. It is important to know how to detect social engineering attacks and be safe from scams.

Business owners should also put security measures to restrict an employee’s ability to use personal devices over business Wi-Fi. While a bring your own device policy may seem harmless and financially prudent on the surface, but, the lack of proper security control measures measures can causes a major risk to businesses.
